Section 1

The Bedrock

We started with the **Operating System** (Bits, Bytes, Hex) and **Mathematical Matureness** (Set theory, Proof by Induction). These tools taught you how to read the language of the computer and the language of the mathematician.

Section 2

Matrices and Rates of Change

**Linear Algebra** showed us that data lives in high-dimensional space. **Calculus** showed us how to move through that space using Gradients and Integrals. Together, they form the engine of Modern Artificial Intelligence.

Section 3

Probability and Statistics

Nature is messy. We learned to quantify that mess using **Distributions**, **Bayes' Theorem**, and **Inference**. This is how you distinguish signal from noise.

Section 4

The Logic of Efficiency

**Algorithms & Data Structures** taught you that *how* you store data determines *how fast* you can use it. From Big O to Dynamic Programming, you learned to optimize the finite resources of the CPU.

Section 5

The Frontiers

Finally, we reached **Theoretical CS**, **Real Analysis**, and **Quantum Computing**. We saw the limits of the Turing model and explored the probability-interference world of the Qubit.
