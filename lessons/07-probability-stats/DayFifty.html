Section 1

Conditional Probability

Conditional probability $P(A|B)$ is the probability that event $A$ occurs, given that $B$ has already occurred.

$$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} $$
üîç Simple: Shrinking the World
Normally, we measure $A$ relative to the entire sample space $S$.
In $P(A|B)$, we change our perspective. $B$ is now our entire world. We only care about the part of $A$ that lives inside $B$.
Section 2

Independence

Two events $A$ and $B$ are independent if the occurrence of one does not change the probability of the other.

$$ A, B \text{ are independent} \iff P(A \cap B) = P(A)P(B) $$ $$ \iff P(A|B) = P(A) $$
‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfall: "Independence" and "Mutually Exclusive" are NOT the same.
Mutually exclusive means they can't happen together. Independence means they don't care about each other.
Section 3

Bayes' Theorem

Bayes' Theorem allows us to "reverse" the conditional probability. If we know $P(B|A)$, we can find $P(A|B)$.

$$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)} $$
  • $P(A|B)$ (Posterior): What we believe after seeing evidence $B$.
  • $P(B|A)$ (Likelihood): How likely $B$ is, assuming $A$ is true.
  • $P(A)$ (Prior): What we believed before seeing evidence.
  • $P(B)$ (Evidence): The total probability of seeing evidence $B$.
üè• Simple: The Virus Test
You test positive for a rare virus ($B$). The test is 99% accurate ($P(B|A)$).
Does that mean you have a 99% chance of having the virus?
No! If the virus only affects 1 in 10,000 people (Prior $P(A)$), most positives will actually be false alarms from healthy people ($P(B|A^c)$).
Section 4

Naive Bayes in Machine Learning

Naive Bayes is a simple probabilistic classifier. It's called "Naive" because it assumes all features are independent of each other given the class.

Spam Filtering

To classify an email as spam:

  1. Count words in Spam vs. Inbox.
  2. Calculate $P(\text{word } | \text{ Spam})$.
  3. Use Bayes' Theorem to find $P(\text{Spam } | \text{ "Buy Now Free Money"})$.
$$ P(C | x_1, \dots, x_n) \propto P(C) \prod_{i=1}^n P(x_i | C) $$

Python: Naive Bayes

Implementing a simple Bayes calculation.

Python Bayes Theorem 
def bayes_theorem(p_a, p_b_given_a, p_b_given_not_a):
    """
    Calculates P(A|B) using Bayes' Theorem.
    P(A): Prior probability of A
    P(B|A): Likelihood of B given A is true
    P(B|not A): Likelihood of B given A is false
    """
    # Calculate P(B) using Law of Total Probability
    # P(B) = P(B|A)*P(A) + P(B|not A)*P(not A)
    p_not_a = 1 - p_a
    p_b = (p_b_given_a * p_a) + (p_b_given_not_a * p_not_a)
    
    # Bayes Formula
    p_a_given_b = (p_b_given_a * p_a) / p_b
    return p_a_given_b

# Example: Disease Testing
# P(Disease) = 0.001 (1 in 1000)
# P(Pos|Disease) = 0.99 (True Positive Rate)
# P(Pos|Healthy) = 0.05 (False Positive Rate)

prob = bayes_theorem(0.001, 0.99, 0.05)
print(f"Probability of disease given positive test: {prob:.4f}")
# Output: 0.0194 (Only ~2% chance!)
