Probability Foundations

Core Axioms (Kolmogorov)

  • $P(A) \geq 0$ for all events A
  • $P(\Omega) = 1$ (sample space has probability 1)
  • $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$ for disjoint events
Bayes' Theorem: The cornerstone of probabilistic inference. $$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)} $$

Key Formulas

  • Complement: $P(A^c) = 1 - P(A)$
  • Addition: $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$
  • Conditional: $P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$
  • Independence: $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B)$
ðŸ”„ Simple: The Medical Test Analogy
Bayes' Theorem is like updating your beliefs. If a medical test says "positive," you flip the question: "Given a positive result, what's the chance I'm actually sick?" depends on both how accurate the test is AND how rare the disease is. That's Bayes!

Random Variables & Moments

Expectation & Variance

$$ E[X] = \sum_x x \cdot P(X=x) \quad \text{or} \quad \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x \cdot f(x)\, dx $$
$$ \text{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2 = E[(X - \mu)^2] $$
Key Properties:
  • $E[aX + b] = aE[X] + b$ (linearity)
  • $\text{Var}(aX + b) = a^2\text{Var}(X)$
  • $E[X + Y] = E[X] + E[Y]$ (always true)
  • $\text{Var}(X + Y) = \text{Var}(X) + \text{Var}(Y) + 2\text{Cov}(X,Y)$

Limit Theorems

Law of Large Numbers (LLN):
As sample size $n \to \infty$, the sample mean $\bar{X}_n \to \mu$ (true mean).
"Average many trials â†’ Expected value"
Central Limit Theorem (CLT):
For i.i.d. samples, the standardized sample mean converges to Normal: $$ \frac{\bar{X}_n - \mu}{\sigma / \sqrt{n}} \xrightarrow{d} N(0, 1) $$
"Sum of many things â†’ Bell curve"

Statistical Inference

Point Estimation

  • MLE: Find $\theta$ that maximizes $P(\text{data} | \theta)$
  • Unbiased: $E[\hat{\theta}] = \theta$

Confidence Intervals

$$ \bar{X} \pm z_{\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} $$

95% CI uses $z_{0.025} = 1.96$

Hypothesis Testing

  • p-value: $P(\text{data this extreme} | H_0)$
  • If $p < \alpha$, reject $H_0$ (statistically significant)
  • Type I Error (Î±): False positive (rejecting true $H_0$)
  • Type II Error (Î²): False negative (missing real effect)
  • Power = 1 - Î²: Probability of catching a real effect
