1. Probability Foundations

Remember Bayes' Theorem: The tool for updating knowledge based on new evidence.

$$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)} $$

2. Random Variables & Distributions

Discrete vs Continuous. Key stats: Expectation (Mean) and Variance.

  • Binomial: Success counts in fixed trials.
  • Poisson: Event counts in fixed time/space.
  • Normal: The ultimate attractor for sums.

3. Limit Theorems

LLN: The average of many trials eventually equals the expected value.

CLT: The distribution of the average eventually looks Normal, no matter where you started.

4. Statistical Inference

MLE: Finding the parameter that makes the observed data most likely.

Confidence Intervals: Estimating a range of values with high certainty.

Hypothesis Testing: Deciding if an effect is statistically significant ($p < \alpha$).
