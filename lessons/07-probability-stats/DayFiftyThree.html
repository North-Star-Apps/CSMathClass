Bernoulli & Binomial

Bernoulli

A single trial with two outcomes (1 = Success, 0 = Failure).

$$ P(X=1) = p, \quad P(X=0) = 1-p $$

Binomial

The number of successes in $n$ independent Bernoulli trials.

$$ P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} $$

üéØ Simple: The Free Throw
A Bernoulli trial is one free throw.
A Binomial variable is the total number of baskets made out of 10 throws.
Section 2

Poisson & Exponential

These two are two sides of the same coin: arrivals.

Poisson (Discrete)

Counts number of events in a fixed interval of time.

$$ P(X=k) = \frac{\lambda^k e^{-\lambda}}{k!} $$

Exponential (Continuous)

Measures time between events.

$$ f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x} $$

üåßÔ∏è Simple: Raindrops on a Tile
The Poisson variable is "How many drops hit this tile in a minute?"
The Exponential variable is "How long do I wait between two consecutive drops?"
Section 3

The Normal (Gaussian)

The "Bell Curve." Thanks to the Central Limit Theorem, this is the most common distribution in nature.

$$ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma})^2} $$
  • Parameters: Mean ($\mu$) and Variance ($\sigma^2$).
  • Standard Normal ($Z$): Normalized with $\mu=0, \sigma=1$.
üìè Simple: Height & Error
Physical measurements (height, weight) and measurement errors usually fall into a Normal distribution. It's the "natural" default for random noise.
Section 4

Geometric & Uniform

Geometric

Number of trials until the first success.

$$ P(X=k) = (1-p)^{k-1} p $$

Uniform

Every outcome in a range $[a, b]$ is equally likely.

$$ f(x) = \frac{1}{b-a} $$

Section 5

Python: Sampling Distributions

Generating random numbers from various distributions.

Python Random Module 
import random
import statistics

# 1. Uniform [a, b]
# Simulating a die roll (continuous)
u = random.uniform(1, 6)

# 2. Normal (Gaussian)
# mu=0, sigma=1 (Standard Normal)
n = random.gauss(0, 1)

# 3. Generating a dataset
random.seed(42) # Reproducible
data = [random.gauss(100, 15) for _ in range(1000)] # IQ scores

print(f"Sample Mean: {statistics.mean(data):.1f}") # ~100.0
print(f"Sample Stdev: {statistics.stdev(data):.1f}") # ~15.0
