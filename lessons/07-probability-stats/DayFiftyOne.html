What is a Random Variable?

A Random Variable ($X$) is a function that maps outcomes from a sample space to real numbers.

$$ X: S \to \mathbb{R} $$
üíª Simple: A Payload Function
Think of the Sample Space as the raw API response.
The Random Variable is a function you write to extract a specific number from that response (e.g., `data.price` or `data.items.length`).
Section 2

Discrete RVs & PMF

Discrete random variables take on specific, separate values (like integers).

Probability Mass Function (PMF): $$ p(x) = P(X = x) $$ $$ \sum p(x) = 1 $$
ü™® Simple: The Pebble Analogy
A discrete distribution is like a set of pebbles placed at specific points on a line. The "weight" of each pebble is its probability.
Section 3

Continuous RVs & PDF

Continuous random variables can take any value in an interval (like the temperature or wait time).

Probability Density Function (PDF): $$ P(a \le X \le b) = \int_a^b f(x) dx $$ $$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) dx = 1 $$
‚ö†Ô∏è Property: For a continuous RV, the probability of hitting a exact single point is zero ($P(X=x) = 0$). We only measure probability over intervals (areas).
üç∞ Simple: The Cake Analogy
A continuous distribution is like a cake spread out on the line. You don't ask about the weight of a 0-width point; you ask about the weight of a slice of the cake.
Section 4

The Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF)

The CDF ($F(x)$) tells you the probability that $X$ is less than or equal to $x$. It works for both discrete and continuous variables.

$$ F(x) = P(X \le x) $$
  • Range: $0$ to $1$.
  • Monotonic: Always non-decreasing.
  • Limits: $\lim_{x \to -\infty} F(x) = 0$ and $\lim_{x \to \infty} F(x) = 1$.
