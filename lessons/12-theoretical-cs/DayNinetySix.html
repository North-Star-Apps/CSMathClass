Section 1

The Ratio α

For an NP-Hard minimization problem, we want an algorithm `A` such that:

Cost(A(x)) ≤ α * Cost(OPT(x))

Where `α` is the approximation ratio (e.g., 2-approximation).

Section 2

Vertex Cover (2-Approx)

Algorithm: Pick an edge (u, v). Add BOTH u and v to cover. Remove all edges touching u or v. Repeat.

This simple greedy algorithm is a 2-Approximation. The true optimal logic is surprisingly elusive (1.36 is hard).
Section 3

TSP (Metric)

If distances satisfy triangle inequality, we can do well.

Christofides Algorithm: Uses MST and Matching to get a 1.5-Approximation.

Without triangle inequality, you can't approximate TSP at all!

Section 4

PCP Theorem

Some problems are hard to even approximate.

Max-Clique: Not approximable within n^(1-ε).

The PCP Theorem (Probabilistically Checkable Proofs) provides the tools to prove these hardness results.
