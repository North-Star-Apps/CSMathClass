Section 1

Product Topology

Finite Products

Basis Elements: Open sets in X × Y are unions of sets U × V where U ∈ τ_X, V ∈ τ_Y. This is the standard topology for ℝ² = ℝ × ℝ.

Infinite Products (Tychonoff)

For arbitrary products Π X_α, the basis elements restrict only finitely many coordinates. Tychonoff's Theorem: The product of ANY collection of compact spaces is compact. (This is a powerhouse theorem in Functional Analysis).
Section 2

Quotient Topology (Gluing)

The Identification Map

Let q: X → X/~ be a surjective map (gluing X according to relation ~). Topology on X/~: U is open in X/~ ⟺ q⁻¹(U) is open in X. In other words: "A set is open downstairs if its preimage is open upstairs."

Classic Examples

• Cylinder: [0,1] × [0,1] gluing (0,y) ~ (1,y). • Mobius Strip: [0,1] × [0,1] gluing (0,y) ~ (1, 1-y) (Twist!). • Torus: Glue both opposite edges.

Visualization: The Torus

A Torus is technically a product space (S¹ × S¹).

Python Parametric Torus 
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Angles (theta, phi)
theta = np.linspace(0, 2*np.pi, 50)
phi = np.linspace(0, 2*np.pi, 50)
theta, phi = np.meshgrid(theta, phi)

# Torus Major radius R, Minor radius r
R, r = 2, 1

# The donut equation
x = (R + r*np.cos(theta)) * np.cos(phi)
y = (R + r*np.cos(theta)) * np.sin(phi)
z = r * np.sin(theta)

# This is effectively S1 x S1 embedded in R3
