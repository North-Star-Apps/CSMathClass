Section 1

Mean Value Theorem

ROLLE'S THEOREM: f continuous on [a,b], differentiable on (a,b) f(a) = f(b) ⟹ ∃c ∈ (a,b): f'(c) = 0 MEAN VALUE THEOREM: f continuous on [a,b], differentiable on (a,b) ⟹ ∃c ∈ (a,b): f'(c) = [f(b) - f(a)] / (b - a)

Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

FTC PART 1: F(x) = ∫ₐˣ f(t) dt ⟹ F'(x) = f(x) (Differentiation undoes integration) FTC PART 2: ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx = F(b) - F(a) where F' = f
Connection: Integration and differentiation are inverse operations!
