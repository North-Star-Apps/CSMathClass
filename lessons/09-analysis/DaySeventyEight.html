Real Analysis Foundations

The Completeness Axiom: Every non-empty set of real numbers that is bounded above has a least upper bound (supremum) in $\mathbb{R}$.

Key Definitions

  • Supremum (sup): Least upper bound of a set
  • Infimum (inf): Greatest lower bound of a set
  • Îµ-Î´ Definition of Limit: $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$ means: $\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists \delta > 0$ such that $|x - a| < \delta \Rightarrow |f(x) - L| < \epsilon$

Why It Matters

The completeness of $\mathbb{R}$ is what separates rationals from reals. Without it, $\sqrt{2}$ doesn't existâ€”a major problem for continuous mathematics!

Sequences & Series

Cauchy Criterion: A sequence $(a_n)$ converges iff $\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists N$ such that $m, n > N \Rightarrow |a_m - a_n| < \epsilon$

Convergence Tests (Series)

Test Condition Result
Ratio Test $\lim |a_{n+1}/a_n| = L$ $L < 1$: converges; $L> 1$: diverges
Root Test $\lim |a_n|^{1/n} = L$ Same as ratio test
Comparison $a_n \leq b_n$ $\sum b_n$ converges â†’ $\sum a_n$ converges

Metric Spaces & Topology

Metric Space $(X, d)$: Set $X$ with distance function $d: X \times X \to \mathbb{R}$
  • $d(x, y) \geq 0$ and $d(x, y) = 0 \iff x = y$
  • $d(x, y) = d(y, x)$ (symmetry)
  • $d(x, z) \leq d(x, y) + d(y, z)$ (triangle inequality)

Key Topological Concepts

  • Open Ball: $B_r(x) = \{y : d(x,y) < r\}$
  • Open Set: Every point has a ball entirely contained in the set
  • Compact: Every open cover has a finite subcover (Heine-Borel: closed + bounded in $\mathbb{R}^n$)
  • Connected: Cannot be split into two disjoint open sets

Measure Theory & Integration

Lebesgue Measure: A way to assign "size" to sets that handles pathological cases where Riemann fails. Key: $\mu([a,b]) = b - a$

Why Lebesgue > Riemann

  • Riemann fails on $f(x) = \mathbb{1}_\mathbb{Q}$ (indicator of rationals)
  • Lebesgue handles this: $\int_0^1 \mathbb{1}_\mathbb{Q} = \mu(\mathbb{Q} \cap [0,1]) = 0$

Dominated Convergence Theorem

If $f_n \to f$ pointwise and $|f_n| \leq g$ for integrable $g$, then:

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \int f_n = \int \lim_{n \to \infty} f_n = \int f $$

This is essential for swapping limits and integrals safely.

Functional Analysis

Hilbert Space: Complete inner product space. Functions become vectors.
$$ \langle f, g \rangle = \int f(x) \overline{g(x)} \, dx $$

Applications

  • Fourier Series: Decompose signals into orthogonal frequencies
  • Kernel Methods: Map data to infinite-dimensional feature spaces
  • Quantum Computing: States are vectors, operators are transformations

Fourier Transform

$$ \hat{f}(\omega) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t) e^{-i\omega t} \, dt $$

Converts time domain to frequency domain. Convolution becomes multiplication!

Information Theory

Shannon Entropy: Measures "surprise" or uncertainty
$$ H(X) = -\sum_x p(x) \log_2 p(x) $$
Units are bits. Maximum when all outcomes equally likely.

Key Quantities

Quantity Formula Meaning
Entropy $H(X)$ $-\sum p \log p$ Uncertainty of X
Joint $H(X,Y)$ $-\sum p(x,y) \log p(x,y)$ Combined uncertainty
Conditional $H(Y|X)$ $H(X,Y) - H(X)$ Uncertainty of Y given X
Mutual Info $I(X;Y)$ $H(X) - H(X|Y)$ Shared information
KL Divergence $\sum p \log(p/q)$ Distance between distributions
