Section 1

Basic Counting Principles

Sum and Product Rules

Sum Rule: A OR B → |A| + |B| (if disjoint) Product Rule: A AND B → |A| × |B| (if independent) Example: • Choose shirt (3) OR pants (4): 3 + 4 = 7 ways • Choose shirt (3) AND pants (4): 3 × 4 = 12 outfits

Inclusion-Exclusion

Inclusion-Exclusion
|A ∪ B| = |A| + |B| - |A ∩ B|
Section 2

Permutations

Ordered arrangements

n! = n × (n-1) × ... × 1 P(n,k) = n!/(n-k)! = ways to arrange k of n items Example: Arrange 3 of 5 books = 5×4×3 = 60

With repetition

MISSISSIPPI has 11 letters with repeats: M:1, I:4, S:4, P:2 Arrangements = 11! / (1! × 4! × 4! × 2!) = 34,650
Section 3

Combinations

Unordered selections

n Choose k
C(n,k) = (n k) = n! / (k!(n-k)!)
Choose 3 from 5: C(5,3) = 5!/(3!2!) = 10 Key identity: C(n,k) = C(n,n-k) Choosing k to take = choosing n-k to leave

Stars and bars

Distribute n identical items into k bins: C(n+k-1, k-1) Example: 10 candies to 3 kids? C(10+3-1, 3-1) = C(12,2) = 66
Section 4

Binomial Theorem

The theorem

Binomial Expansion
(x+y)ⁿ = Σₖ C(n,k) xᵏ yⁿ⁻ᵏ

Key identities

• C(n,0) + C(n,1) + ... + C(n,n) = 2ⁿ (subsets of n elements) • C(n,k) = C(n-1,k-1) + C(n-1,k) (Pascal's identity) • Pascal's triangle: each entry = sum of two above
